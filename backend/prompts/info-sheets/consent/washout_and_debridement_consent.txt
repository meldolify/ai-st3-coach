Patient: Mrs Sandra Yeboah, 42 years old
Procedure: Wound Washout and Debridement of Open Fracture Right Forearm
Context: Presented to A and E after a fall from a ladder at home three hours ago sustaining a Gustilo grade 3A open fracture of the right radius with a 6 centimetre wound over the volar forearm with exposed bone and contamination with soil. I V antibiotics and tetanus prophylaxis have been given. The wound has been irrigated and dressed with a saline soaked dressing. She has been listed on the emergency trauma list for formal washout and debridement within six hours of injury. She is haemodynamically stable and alert.
Patient Expectations: Mrs Yeboah knows her arm is broken and can see the wound. She is frightened by the appearance of the injury and in pain. She has been told she needs an urgent operation to clean the wound and fix the bone. She wants to know if she will lose her arm, how many operations she might need, and whether the wound will need a skin graft.
