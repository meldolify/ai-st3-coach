Patient: Mr Liam Brennan, 24 years old
Procedure: Emergency Four Compartment Fasciotomy of the Right Lower Leg
Context: Admitted via A and E with a closed tibial shaft fracture following a motorcycle accident four hours ago. The fracture has been reduced and placed in a backslab. Over the past two hours he has developed escalating pain not controlled by I V morphine, pain on passive extension of the toes, and a tense swollen right calf. The orthopaedic registrar has referred him to plastics with suspected compartment syndrome. He has been listed for emergency fasciotomy. His parents are present.
Patient Expectations: Mr Brennan is in severe pain and distressed. He was told his leg fracture had been dealt with and does not understand why he now needs another emergency operation. He wants to know what compartment syndrome is, whether he could lose his leg, and why the operation cannot wait until the morning.
