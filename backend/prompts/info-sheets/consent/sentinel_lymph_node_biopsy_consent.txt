Patient: Mrs Sarah Mitchell, 44 years old
Procedure: Wide Local Excision of Left Calf Melanoma and Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy
Context: Mrs Mitchell was referred urgently by her G P with a changing pigmented mole on her left calf. Excision biopsy confirmed a malignant melanoma with a Breslow thickness of 2.1 millimetres. After discussion at the skin cancer multidisciplinary team meeting, she has been listed for wide local excision with a one centimetre margin and sentinel lymph node biopsy of the left groin. She was seen in the plastic surgery clinic two weeks ago and the procedure was outlined. She has attended today for the operation. She is otherwise fit and well.
Patient Expectations: Mrs Mitchell understands she needs a bigger operation to remove more skin around where the mole was, but she does not understand the sentinel lymph node biopsy. She has looked online and is frightened about the possibility of the cancer having spread. She wants to know what the sentinel node test actually involves, what happens if it is positive, and whether she will need further treatment.
