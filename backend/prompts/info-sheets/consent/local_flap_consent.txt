Patient: Mr George Patterson, 79 years old
Procedure: Excision of Squamous Cell Carcinoma Right Temple and Local Flap Reconstruction
Context: Mr Patterson was referred urgently by his G P with a growing, crusting lesion on his right temple. A punch biopsy confirmed a squamous cell carcinoma. He was seen in the plastic surgery clinic two weeks ago and listed for wide local excision with a local flap closure. He has attended today for the operation under local anaesthetic. He has a history of type 2 diabetes, takes metformin, and is otherwise well.
Patient Expectations: Mr Patterson has been told the lump on his temple is a skin cancer and needs to be cut out with an operation to cover the area. He does not understand what a local flap is and is worried about having surgery on his temple near his eye. He wants to know whether it will affect his vision, how big the scar will be, and whether the cancer could come back.
