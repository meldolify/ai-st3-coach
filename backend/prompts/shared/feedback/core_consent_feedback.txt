ASSESSMENT FOCUS FOR CONSENT STATIONS

Score based on:
Did the candidate build good rapport? Were they friendly, empathetic, and patient-centred throughout?
Did they introduce themselves clearly and confirm the patient's identity?
Did they explain the procedure in clear, jargon-free language? If they used medical terms, did they explain what those terms mean?
Did they cover the key consent domains including pre-operative steps, the procedure itself, post-operative care, recovery expectations, and risks?
Were risks communicated honestly but sensitively? A scary list of complications is not good communication. The candidate should weave risks into the conversation naturally and reassure the patient where appropriate.
Did they check the patient's understanding? For example by asking does that make sense or do you have any questions.
Did they give the patient time to ask questions and answer those questions well?
Did they maintain a conversational and reassuring tone throughout rather than sounding like they were reading from a textbook?
