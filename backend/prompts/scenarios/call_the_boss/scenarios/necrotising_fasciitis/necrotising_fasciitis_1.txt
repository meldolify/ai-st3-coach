SCENARIO TITLE

Necrotising Fasciitis

PROBING QUESTIONS

(Internal reference only. Questions the consultant may ask during the call.
Use these to challenge or guide the candidate after they finish presenting.
Delivery specificity is controlled by the personality layer.)

"What makes you think this is necrotising fasciitis rather than severe cellulitis?"
"What is the L R I N E C score and what did it come back as?"
"Have you started the Sepsis Six bundle?"
"What antibiotics are you going to use and why?"
"Who else have you contacted — anaesthetics, I T U, theatres?"
"Talk me through what you would do in theatre step by step."
"What would you do if we get to theatre and the tissue looks viable?"
"How would you manage the wound post-operatively?"

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

Structured clinical presentation using S B A R or equivalent framework
The candidate must introduce themselves clearly, state their role, and identify the consultant.
The candidate must present the case in a logical structured manner covering situation, background, assessment, and recommendation.
The candidate must convey the urgency of the situation clearly and early in the call.

Recognition of necrotising fasciitis as a life-threatening surgical emergency
The candidate must identify that this presentation is consistent with necrotising fasciitis based on the rapid spread, systemic toxicity, and failure to respond to antibiotics.
The candidate must state that necrotising fasciitis is a clinical diagnosis and a surgical emergency.
Knowing that key clinical features include erythema spreading beyond markings, pain out of proportion, blistering, dusky discolouration, crepitus, and systemic sepsis.
Knowing that the L R I N E C score of 9 places the patient in the high-risk category.

Sepsis recognition and resuscitation
The candidate must recognise that the patient is in septic shock with hypotension, tachycardia, raised lactate, oliguria, and confusion.
The candidate must mention initiating or completing the Sepsis Six bundle: blood cultures, lactate, urine output monitoring, I V fluids, I V antibiotics, and high-flow oxygen.
The candidate must mention ongoing fluid resuscitation and monitoring of urine output.

Antibiotic strategy and escalation from medical management
The candidate must recognise that co-amoxiclav alone is insufficient for necrotising fasciitis.
The candidate must recommend escalation to broad-spectrum triple antibiotic therapy covering gram-positive, gram-negative, and anaerobic organisms. An appropriate regimen includes benzylpenicillin, clindamycin, and gentamicin, or alternative combinations supported by stating this is per trust guidelines.
Knowing that clindamycin has a specific role in necrotising fasciitis as it inhibits toxin production.

Surgical planning including debridement approach
The candidate must state that the patient needs urgent surgical debridement tonight and request the consultant to come in.
The candidate must describe informing C E P O D theatres, the on-call anaesthetist, and I T U.
The candidate must mention consent or consent form 4 if the patient lacks capacity, and informing the next of kin.
The candidate must describe the surgical approach: wide excision of all necrotic tissue, extending until healthy bleeding tissue is encountered, copious washout, tissue samples for microbiology and histology, and wound coverage with V A C or moist dressings.

Post-operative care including I T U and second look planning
The candidate must arrange post-operative I T U care.
The candidate must plan a second look in theatre within 48 hours for further debridement.
The candidate must mention ongoing antibiotic therapy tailored to microbiology results.

BONUS MARKING POINTS

Structured clinical presentation using S B A R or equivalent framework
The candidate demonstrates a calm and professional telephone manner despite the urgency.
The candidate anticipates the consultant's likely questions and proactively addresses them.

Recognition of necrotising fasciitis as a life-threatening surgical emergency
The candidate discusses the differential diagnosis including severe cellulitis but states they are treating as necrotising fasciitis until proven otherwise.
The candidate mentions risk factors in this patient: poorly controlled diabetes, immunocompromised state, and chronic kidney disease.
The candidate mentions the finger sweep test as a diagnostic adjunct if clinically uncertain, with the caveat that this would be discussed with the consultant before performing.
Knowing that Type 1 necrotising fasciitis is polymicrobial and Type 2 is monomicrobial, typically Group A Streptococcus.

Sepsis recognition and resuscitation
The candidate mentions involving the critical care outreach team or medical registrar for ongoing resuscitation while preparing for theatre.
The candidate discusses the need for vasopressor support if the patient remains hypotensive despite fluid resuscitation.

Antibiotic strategy and escalation from medical management
The candidate discusses contacting the on-call microbiologist for guidance on antibiotic choice.
The candidate mentions that tissue samples sent from theatre are more valuable than wound swabs for guiding antibiotic therapy.

Surgical planning including debridement approach
The candidate describes the pathognomonic finding of dishwater-grey fluid at the fascial plane.
The candidate mentions that healthy fascia should resist finger dissection whereas necrotic fascia separates easily.
The candidate discusses the potential need for extensive skin loss and future reconstructive options including skin grafting and flap coverage.

Post-operative care including I T U and second look planning
The candidate mentions monitoring for complications including acute kidney injury, multi-organ failure, and disseminated intravascular coagulation.
The candidate discusses the high mortality rate of necrotising fasciitis, which ranges from 20 to 40 percent even with optimal management.
The candidate mentions the importance of thorough documentation and clear handover.

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.

END OF SCENARIO CRITERIA
