SCENARIO TITLE

Open Fracture of the Lower Limb

PROBING QUESTIONS

(Internal reference only. Questions the consultant may ask during the call.
Use these to challenge or guide the candidate after they finish presenting.
Delivery specificity is controlled by the personality layer.)

"What Gustilo-Anderson grade is this?"
"Does this patient need to go to theatre tonight or can it wait until tomorrow?"
"What are the B A P R A S lower limb guidelines?"
"Are the orthopaedic team aware? What are they planning?"
"Who needs to be present in theatre for this case?"
"Are you worried about compartment syndrome?"
"What is your plan for soft tissue coverage?"
"What would you do in the wound tonight in A and E?"

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

Structured clinical presentation covering the polytrauma and the lower limb injury
The candidate must present the polytrauma clearly including the pneumothorax and the lower limb injury, distinguishing the life-threatening injuries from the limb-threatening injury.
The candidate must describe the lower limb injury accurately: location, extent of soft tissue loss, exposed bone, periosteal stripping, and neurovascular status.
The candidate must inform the consultant and let them know they will need to be available for a joint case with orthopaedics.

Gustilo-Anderson classification and B A P R A S guidelines
The candidate must classify this as a Gustilo-Anderson type 3B injury: extensive soft tissue damage with periosteal stripping, exposed bone, and significant soft tissue loss requiring flap coverage.
The candidate must know the B A P R A S and B O A standards for the management of open lower limb fractures, including the requirement for joint care by orthopaedic and plastic surgery consultants.
The candidate must know that surgery should be performed by a consultant plastic surgeon and a consultant orthopaedic surgeon.

Assessment of neurovascular status and compartment syndrome risk
The candidate must confirm that pulses are present and the foot is sensate, which is reassuring.
The candidate must state that the patient needs regular monitoring for evolving compartment syndrome, especially after reduction.
The candidate must recognise that compartment syndrome cannot be fully excluded at initial assessment.

Immediate wound management and antibiotic strategy
The candidate must state that the wound should not be debrided or irrigated in A and E, only covered with a sterile moist dressing or clingfilm after removal of gross contaminants.
The candidate must commence I V antibiotics: co-amoxiclav is first line (or teicoplanin if penicillin allergic).
The candidate must give a tetanus booster.
The candidate must mention medical photography of the wound.
The candidate must state the wound should not be repeatedly uncovered for viewing.

Joint ortho-plastic surgical planning
The candidate must state the patient should be admitted under joint care of the orthopaedic and plastic surgery teams.
The candidate must describe the surgical plan: in theatre, thorough debridement with pulse lavage, removal of devitalised tissue and contaminants, assessment for exposed vessels, and orthopaedic fixation with an external fixator.
The candidate must plan for V A C dressing after initial debridement.
The candidate must plan for a return to theatre within 48 hours for a second look.

Soft tissue reconstruction strategy and timeline
The candidate must know that soft tissue coverage should ideally be achieved within 72 hours, and no later than 7 days (the B A P R A S fix and flap principle).
The candidate must discuss reconstructive options based on the location of the defect: upper third — gastrocnemius flap, middle third — soleus flap, lower third — free flap.
The candidate must mention that at the point of definitive soft tissue closure the orthopaedic team would convert to internal fixation.

BONUS MARKING POINTS

Structured clinical presentation covering the polytrauma and the lower limb injury
The candidate discusses whether the patient needs to go to theatre tonight, demonstrating clinical reasoning. In this case, there is no devascularised limb, no heavy contamination, and no compartment syndrome, so the case can be scheduled for a dedicated trauma list tomorrow with consultant presence.
The candidate mentions the M E S S score (Mangled Extremity Severity Score) as an adjunct for assessing limb salvageability.

Gustilo-Anderson classification and B A P R A S guidelines
The candidate knows the full Gustilo-Anderson classification including 3A (adequate soft tissue), 3B (requiring flap), and 3C (vascular injury requiring repair).
The candidate mentions other classification systems such as the A O classification or the Limb Salvage Index.

Assessment of neurovascular status and compartment syndrome risk
The candidate mentions that compartment pressures should be checked after orthopaedic reduction.
The candidate mentions requesting regular neurovascular observations by nursing staff overnight.

Immediate wound management and antibiotic strategy
The candidate mentions that antibiotics should be given within 1 hour of presentation to A and E as per the B A P R A S guidelines.
The candidate discusses the evidence base for antibiotic choice in open fractures.

Joint ortho-plastic surgical planning
The candidate mentions the need to optimise the patient for theatre: complete the chest drain, ensure haemodynamic stability, arrange anaesthetic review, and book a dedicated trauma list.
The candidate discusses the role of pulse lavage versus low-pressure irrigation in theatre.

Soft tissue reconstruction strategy and timeline
The candidate discusses specific flap options for this mid-shaft tibial defect in more detail, including local muscle flaps and free flaps.
The candidate mentions the importance of a joint pre-operative planning discussion between the orthopaedic and plastic surgery consultants.

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.

END OF SCENARIO CRITERIA

Once the main communication and clinical domains have been reasonably covered, or you judge that the scenario time has elapsed, end the scenario naturally.
