SCENARIO TITLE

Lower Limb Reconstruction

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

You are called to the emergency department to see a thirty-five year old man who has been involved in a motorcycle accident. He has an open fracture of his right tibia with a large soft tissue defect over the anteromedial aspect of the mid-shaft. The orthopaedic team have classified this as a Gustilo Anderson grade three B injury and have performed initial wound washout and external fixation.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Gustilo Anderson classification:
Type one: wound less than one centimetre, minimal contamination, simple fracture pattern
Type two: wound one to ten centimetres, moderate soft tissue damage, no flap required
Type three A: adequate soft tissue coverage despite extensive wound, often high energy
Type three B: inadequate soft tissue coverage requiring flap reconstruction
Type three C: associated vascular injury requiring repair

Initial management:
A T L S principles — primary survey first
Photograph wound, apply saline-soaked dressing, splint limb
Intravenous antibiotics within one hour (co-amoxiclav, add gentamicin for gross contamination)
Tetanus prophylaxis
Joint ortho-plastic assessment within twenty-four hours
Surgical debridement within twelve to twenty-four hours ideally

Timing:
B A P N (British Association of Plastic Surgeons and British Orthopaedic Association) guidelines
Definitive soft tissue cover within seventy-two hours if possible, and certainly within seven days
Early cover reduces infection rates and improves outcomes
Serial debridement may be needed before definitive cover

Reconstructive options by zone:
Proximal third: gastrocnemius muscle flap (medial head most commonly)
Middle third: soleus muscle flap (can be hemisoleus), or free flap
Distal third: free flap is usually required (no reliable local muscle flaps)
Common free flaps: A L T (anterolateral thigh), gracilis, latissimus dorsi, rectus abdominis

Free flap considerations:
Recipient vessels: posterior tibial or anterior tibial artery (end-to-side preferred to preserve distal flow)
C T angiography to assess vessel status pre-operatively
Muscle flaps preferred for dead space obliteration and infection control
Fasciocutaneous flaps (A L T) useful for surface coverage without bulk

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Discussion of the B O A / B A P N standards for major trauma centres
Negative pressure wound therapy as bridge to definitive cover
Antibiotic cement spacers or beads for contaminated wounds
Ilizarov frame or bone transport for segmental bone loss
Masquelet technique (induced membrane) for bone defects
Discussion of limb salvage versus primary amputation — M E S S score (Mangled Extremity Severity Score) has poor positive predictive value, decision should be clinical and M D T based
Role of the orthoplastic combined approach in improving outcomes
Chimeric flaps incorporating bone (fibula) for composite defects

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
