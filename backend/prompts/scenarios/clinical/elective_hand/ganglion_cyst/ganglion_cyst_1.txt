SCENARIO TITLE

Ganglion Cyst — Painless Dorsal Wrist Lump

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A 28-year-old woman presents with a painless lump on the back of her right wrist that she first noticed about three months ago. It has gradually increased in size. She works in an office and finds it cosmetically bothersome. She reports it occasionally aches after prolonged typing. On examination there is a firm, smooth, 2 centimetre transilluminant swelling over the dorsum of the wrist at the scapholunate interval.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Pathology:
Mucin-filled cyst arising from joint capsule or tendon sheath
Not a true cyst as it lacks a synovial lining
Contains thick clear viscous fluid
One-way valve mechanism allows filling but not drainage

Common locations:
Dorsal wrist at the scapholunate interval — 60 to 70 percent of all wrist ganglions
Volar wrist at the radiocarpal joint or scaphotrapezial joint
Flexor tendon sheath (volar retinacular cyst) at the A1 pulley
Mucous cyst at the D I P J is a separate entity

Clinical assessment:
Firm, smooth, well-defined swelling
Transillumination positive
Fixed to deep structures but mobile over overlying skin
Assess wrist range of motion
Check for scapholunate instability as an associated finding

Differential diagnosis:
Giant cell tumour of tendon sheath
Lipoma
Epidermal inclusion cyst
Carpal boss (bony prominence at second or third C M C joint)
Sarcoma (rare but must consider in any soft tissue lump)

Investigations:
Ultrasound confirms cystic nature and defines anatomy
X-ray if bony pathology suspected such as carpal boss
M R I for occult ganglion not clinically apparent or to exclude other pathology

Conservative management:
Reassurance that ganglions are benign and up to 50 percent resolve spontaneously
Aspiration with large bore needle, recurrence rate approximately 50 percent
Aspiration more successful for volar retinacular cysts
No role for the historical bible technique

Surgical excision:
Transverse or longitudinal incision over the ganglion
Trace the stalk to its origin at the joint capsule
Excise a cuff of capsule around the stalk attachment
Dorsal ganglion: protect extensor tendons and posterior interosseous nerve terminal branch
Volar ganglion: protect radial artery which is intimately related to the cyst
Recurrence rate approximately 5 to 10 percent after surgical excision

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Arthroscopic ganglion excision for dorsal wrist ganglions
Occult ganglion diagnosed on M R I presenting with dorsal wrist pain without a visible lump
Volar retinacular cysts often present as a small painful lump at the base of the finger
Intra-osseous ganglion as a rare variant
Risk of scapholunate ligament damage during dorsal ganglion excision
Association between dorsal ganglion and underlying scapholunate pathology

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
