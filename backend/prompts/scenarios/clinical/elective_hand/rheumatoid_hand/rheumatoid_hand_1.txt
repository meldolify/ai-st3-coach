SCENARIO TITLE

Rheumatoid Hand — Ulnar Drift and Swan Neck Deformities

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A 48-year-old woman with a 12-year history of rheumatoid arthritis is referred to your hand clinic by her rheumatologist. Despite being on biologic therapy, she has developed progressive deformities in both hands. She has difficulty with grip and pinch and is struggling with daily activities. On examination she has ulnar drift at the M C P joints bilaterally, swan neck deformities of several fingers, and subluxation of the M C P joints of her right hand with prominent metacarpal heads on the dorsum.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Pathomechanics of ulnar drift:
Synovitis stretches the sagittal bands and joint capsule
Extensor tendons sublux ulnarly between metacarpal heads
Radial collateral ligaments attenuate
Intrinsic tightness and flexor tendon pull contribute
Wrist radial deviation creates a zig-zag pattern with M C P J ulnar drift

Swan neck deformity:
P I P J hyperextension with D I P J flexion
Causes: F D S rupture, intrinsic tightness, P I P J dorsal capsule laxity, mallet deformity at D I P J
Nalebuff classification: Type 1 flexible in all positions, Type 2 P I P J flexion limited by intrinsic tightness, Type 3 limited in all positions, Type 4 stiff with joint destruction

Boutonniere deformity:
P I P J flexion with D I P J hyperextension
Central slip attenuation allows P I P J to flex
Lateral bands migrate volarly and become D I P J extensors causing hyperextension
Stages: passively correctable, fixed P I P J contracture, fixed with joint destruction

Systematic assessment:
Examine wrist, M C P J, P I P J, D I P J, and thumb systematically
Assess tendon integrity: check individual E D C, E P L function
Look for extensor tendon ruptures: Vaughan-Jackson pattern starting with E D M
Check flexor tendons for Mannerfelt lesion (F P L rupture on scaphoid spur)
Assess skin quality, vascularity, and sensibility

Medical management:
Optimise disease-modifying therapy with rheumatology before surgery
Biologic agents should be held peri-operatively per rheumatology guidance
Surgery is reconstructive not curative of the disease process

Surgical priorities (Nalebuff):
1. Treat the wrist first as the keystone
2. Then M C P joints
3. Then P I P J and D I P J
4. Tendon reconstruction before joint reconstruction
Address tendon ruptures urgently

M C P J surgery:
Silicone arthroplasty (Swanson) remains the gold standard for M C P J disease
Centralise extensor tendons, rebalance soft tissues
Crossed intrinsic transfer for ulnar drift correction
Results in pain relief and improved cosmesis, moderate arc of motion

Tendon surgery:
Extensor tendon rupture: end-to-side transfer to adjacent intact tendon, or E I P transfer
Flexor tendon rupture (Mannerfelt): F D S transfer or bridge graft
Synovectomy and tenosynovectomy for impending rupture
Darrach procedure or Sauve-Kapandji for distal ulna head involvement causing tendon attrition
