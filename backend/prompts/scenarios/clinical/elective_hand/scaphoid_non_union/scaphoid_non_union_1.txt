SCENARIO TITLE

Scaphoid Non-Union — Persistent Radial Wrist Pain

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A 25-year-old right-hand-dominant man presents with persistent pain in his right wrist that has been present for about eight months. He recalls falling onto his outstretched hand while playing football but did not seek medical attention at the time. The pain is in the anatomical snuffbox area and worsens with gripping. X-rays show a scaphoid waist fracture non-union with a cystic change at the fracture site and no evidence of avascular necrosis.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Anatomy and blood supply:
Scaphoid bridges the proximal and distal carpal rows
Blood supply predominantly from the dorsal carpal branch of the radial artery
Enters through the dorsal ridge supplying the proximal 70 to 80 percent in a retrograde fashion
Proximal pole fractures at highest risk of avascular necrosis due to this retrograde supply

Reasons for non-union:
Missed diagnosis and delayed presentation
Proximal pole fractures with precarious blood supply
Inadequate immobilisation
Fracture displacement greater than 1 millimetre
Smoking

Consequences of untreated non-union:
Predictable pattern of degenerative arthritis called S N A C wrist
Scaphoid non-union advanced collapse
Stage 1: radial styloid arthritis
Stage 2: scaphoid fossa and radioscaphoid arthritis
Stage 3: capitolunate arthritis
Stage 4: pan-carpal arthritis
Carpal collapse with D I S I pattern (dorsal intercalated segment instability)

Imaging:
Plain X-rays in four views including scaphoid views
C T scan with sagittal reconstructions to assess union, cyst size, and humpback deformity
M R I to assess proximal pole vascularity: loss of marrow signal on T1 suggests avascular necrosis
Intrascaphoid angle measurement for humpback deformity

Surgical options for non-union without arthritis:
Non-vascularised bone graft (Matti-Russe or wedge graft from iliac crest or distal radius)
Headless compression screw fixation (Herbert screw or Acutrak)
Correct humpback deformity with volar wedge graft to restore scaphoid length and alignment
Vascularised bone graft for proximal pole avascular necrosis

Vascularised bone graft options:
1,2 intercompartmental supraretinacular artery (1,2 I C S R A) pedicled graft from distal radius (Zaidemberg)
Volar carpal artery pedicled graft
Free medial femoral condyle vascularised graft for failed pedicled graft
Indicated when M R I shows proximal pole avascular necrosis

Salvage for S N A C wrist:
Radial styloidectomy for Stage 1
Scaphoid excision and four-corner fusion for Stage 2 and 3
Proximal row carpectomy for Stage 2 (if capitate head articular surface preserved)
Total wrist arthrodesis for Stage 4 or pan-carpal disease
