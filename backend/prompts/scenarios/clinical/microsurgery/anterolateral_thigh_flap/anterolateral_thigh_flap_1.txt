SCENARIO TITLE

A L T Flap for Tongue Reconstruction

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A forty year old man has undergone excision of a squamous cell carcinoma involving the lateral tongue and floor of mouth. The resultant defect requires soft tissue reconstruction. You have been asked to discuss your approach to reconstruction using an anterolateral thigh flap. Please discuss this case with the examiner.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Anatomy:
- Based on descending branch of lateral circumflex femoral artery
- Perforators emerge through or between vastus lateralis and rectus femoris
- Septocutaneous perforators easier to harvest than musculocutaneous
- Flap centred on a line from A S I S to superolateral patella
- Pedicle length typically 8 to 12 centimetres, artery calibre 2 to 3 millimetres
- Can be harvested as fasciocutaneous or including vastus lateralis muscle cuff

Defect Assessment:
- Size and subsite of oral defect determines flap volume and design
- Hemiglossectomy: need bulk for tongue mobility, swallowing, and speech
- Consider whether sensate flap needed (lateral cutaneous nerve of thigh)

Recipient Vessels:
- Superior thyroid artery or facial artery
- Internal jugular vein or facial vein or external jugular vein
- End-to-end or end-to-side anastomosis depending on vessel availability
- Consider vein grafts if neck dissection has depleted vessels

Flap Inset:
- Flap folded or de-epithelialised to recreate tongue bulk
- Suture to tongue remnant allowing adequate oral competence
- Avoid excessive bulk causing airway compromise
- May require tracheostomy in immediate post-operative period

Post-operative Monitoring:
- Hourly flap checks: colour, capillary refill, Doppler signal
- Intraoral flaps harder to monitor: consider externalised monitor paddle
- Keep head of bed elevated, avoid neck flexion or compression

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

- Chimeric A L T flap with vastus lateralis for composite defects
- Perforator-based propeller variant for smaller defects
- Thinning techniques for A L T in thin patients
- Comparison with radial forearm free flap for tongue reconstruction
- Functional outcomes: speech and swallowing rehabilitation
- Role of lateral cutaneous nerve of thigh for sensate reconstruction
- Backup plan: convert to anteromedial thigh flap if no A L T perforators found
