SCENARIO TITLE

Fibula Free Flap for Mandible Reconstruction

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A fifty-five year old man has undergone segmental resection of the mandible for a squamous cell carcinoma involving the body of the mandible. You are planning reconstruction using a fibula free flap. Please discuss your approach with the examiner.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Anatomy:
- Peroneal artery and venae comitantes supply the fibula via periosteal and endosteal blood supply
- Skin paddle supplied by septocutaneous or musculocutaneous perforators through lateral compartment
- Up to 25 centimetres of bone available for harvest
- Must preserve 6 to 8 centimetres distally to maintain ankle stability
- Nutrient artery enters mid-shaft; periosteal blood supply allows multiple osteotomies

Pre-operative Assessment:
- C T angiography or M R angiography of lower limb to confirm three-vessel run-off to foot
- Absent or dominant peroneal artery (peronea magna) is a contraindication
- Assess peripheral vascular disease, previous leg trauma or surgery
- Virtual surgical planning with C T for osteotomy guides and plate prebending

Harvest Technique:
- Lateral approach between peroneus longus and soleus
- Identify and protect common peroneal nerve at fibula neck
- Subperiosteal dissection preserving periosteal blood supply
- Osteotomies performed with reciprocating saw, closing wedge technique
- Skin paddle centred over posterior crural septum

Mandible Reconstruction:
- Reconstruction plate or miniplates for fixation
- Closing wedge osteotomies to recreate mandibular angle and contour
- Fibula positioned at alveolar height for future dental implants
- End-to-end or end-to-side anastomosis to facial artery and vein or external carotid branches

Donor Site:
- Primary closure of skin if paddle is small
- S S G for larger skin paddle donor defects
- Ankle instability prevented by preserving distal 6 to 8 centimetres
- Compartment syndrome is a rare but serious complication
- Peroneal nerve injury causing foot drop

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

- Double-barrel fibula technique for increased bone height
- Virtual surgical planning with patient-specific cutting guides and plates
- Flow-through fibula flap maintaining peroneal artery continuity
- Comparison with scapula free flap, iliac crest free flap, and reconstruction plate alone
- Secondary procedures: implant-borne dental prostheses on fibula
- Osteoradionecrosis as indication for fibula reconstruction
- Role of I C G angiography for skin paddle perfusion assessment
