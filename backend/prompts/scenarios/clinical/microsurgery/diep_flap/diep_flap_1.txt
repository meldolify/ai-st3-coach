SCENARIO TITLE

D I E P Flap Breast Reconstruction

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

You are called to see a fifty year old woman in the breast clinic. She has recently been diagnosed with left breast cancer and is scheduled for mastectomy. She has been referred to you to discuss options for autologous breast reconstruction, and she is particularly interested in the D I E P flap. Please discuss this case with the examiner.

MAIN CLINICAL DOMAINS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

- Indications and patient selection for D I E P flap
- Pre-operative assessment and imaging
- Surgical anatomy of deep inferior epigastric perforators
- Flap harvest technique and comparison with T R A M
- Recipient vessel selection and microvascular anastomosis
- Post-operative monitoring and flap salvage
- Complications and their management

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Patient Selection and Assessment:
- B M I, smoking status, previous abdominal surgery, body habitus
- C T angiography to map perforator location, calibre, and intramuscular course
- Allen test equivalent: assess dominance of perforator system
- Discuss with oncology regarding adjuvant therapy timing

Surgical Anatomy:
- Deep inferior epigastric artery arising from external iliac
- Perforators pass through rectus abdominis muscle to supply overlying skin and fat
- Mathes-Nahai Type 3 muscle classification for rectus abdominis (two dominant pedicles)
- Harvest zones: zone 1 ipsilateral periumbilical most reliable, zone 4 contralateral lateral least reliable

D I E P versus T R A M:
- D I E P preserves rectus abdominis muscle and anterior rectus sheath
- Reduced donor site morbidity: lower abdominal wall hernia and bulge rates
- Longer dissection time due to intramuscular perforator dissection
- T R A M options: pedicled (superior epigastric), free muscle-sparing, or full muscle

Recipient Vessels:
- Internal mammary artery and vein preferred (I M A)
- Typically third or fourth rib cartilage removed for access
- Alternative: thoracodorsal vessels

Post-operative Monitoring:
- Hourly flap checks for first 24 to 48 hours
- Assess colour, capillary refill time, turgor, temperature, handheld Doppler
- Venous congestion: blue or purple discolouration, brisk dark capillary refill, raised turgor
- Arterial insufficiency: pale or white, absent capillary refill, reduced turgor
- Implantable Doppler monitoring as adjunct
- Threshold for return to theatre: any signs of vascular compromise warrant urgent exploration

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

- Stacked or bipedicled D I E P for large volume reconstruction
- Neurotisation of flap using intercostal nerve to restore sensation
- Lymphoedema risk and sentinel node considerations
- Discussion of timing: immediate versus delayed reconstruction
- Fat necrosis rates related to perfusion zones
- Supercharging or turbocharging techniques for zone 3 perfusion
- Use of indocyanine green angiography intra-operatively to assess perfusion

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
