SCENARIO TITLE

Latissimus Dorsi Flap Breast Reconstruction

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A fifty-five year old woman is attending your clinic following a left mastectomy performed six months ago. She is interested in delayed breast reconstruction and has been counselled about the latissimus dorsi flap with implant. She has had previous abdominal surgery making a D I E P flap unsuitable. Please discuss this case with the examiner.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Anatomy and Classification:
- Mathes-Nahai Type 5 muscle: one dominant pedicle (thoracodorsal) plus segmental secondary pedicles (posterior intercostals, lumbar perforators)
- Thoracodorsal artery from subscapular artery, branch of third part of axillary artery
- Thoracodorsal nerve: long thoracic nerve of Bell is nearby but distinct
- Muscle origin from spinous processes T7 to L5, iliac crest, lower ribs
- Large surface area, reliable blood supply, long pedicle arc of rotation

Pedicled versus Free:
- Most commonly used as pedicled flap for breast reconstruction
- Tunnelled subcutaneously around the lateral chest wall
- Can be used as free flap if thoracodorsal pedicle compromised
- Pedicled version avoids microvascular anastomosis

Implant Combination:
- Latissimus dorsi alone rarely provides sufficient volume for breast reconstruction
- Implant or tissue expander placed beneath the flap and pectoralis major
- Provides well-vascularised muscle cover for the implant
- Reduces capsular contracture rates compared to implant-only reconstruction

Donor Site:
- Seroma is the most common complication (up to 30 to 40 percent)
- Managed with drains, quilting sutures, or aspiration
- Shoulder weakness: rarely functionally significant in most patients
- Scar position in bra line or along back

Contraindications:
- Previous thoracodorsal vessel damage (axillary surgery or radiotherapy)
- Prior thoracotomy through the muscle
- Need for crutch mobilisation (latissimus required for crutch walking)

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

- Extended latissimus dorsi flap (harvesting extra fat) to avoid implant
- Thoracodorsal artery perforator (T A P) flap as muscle-sparing variant
- Role in chest wall reconstruction for radiation ulcers
- Use in head and neck, upper and lower limb reconstruction
- Functional transfer for elbow flexion (Steindler) or finger flexion
- Muscle denervation to prevent animation deformity
- Comparison with T R A M and D I E P for patients with available abdominal tissue

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
