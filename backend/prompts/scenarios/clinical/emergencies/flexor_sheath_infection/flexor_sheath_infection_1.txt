ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Diagnosis and urgency
Flexor sheath infection or flexor tenosynovitis which is a clinical emergency.

History
Patient's age, hand dominance, occupation, smoking history, no recall of injury, possible thorn injury, onset and progression of symptoms, past medical history and drug history.

Examination
Kanavel signs including: fusiform swelling, flexion attitude, pain on passive extension and tenderness along the flexor sheath.

Investigations
Blood tests and x-ray.

Initial management
Broad spectrum antibiotics for example Co-amoxiclav.

Definitive management
Urgent surgery for flexor sheath washout.

Surgical steps
Incision over A1 and A5 pulley, wash by saline using venflon cannula.

Post-operative care
Arrange for second look in 48 hours.



BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)


Making sure that the patient is hemodynamically stable and rule out sepsis.

History
Personal history including patient's age, hand dominance, occupation, smoking history, tetanus status and last meal, social support. More details around any potential injury like the timing of injury, mechanism, onset and progression of symptoms, any treatment given including antibiotics. Past medical history of the patient and drug history.

Examination
Kanavel signs (fusiform swelling, flexion attitude, pain on passive extension and tenderness along the flexor sheath), neurovascular status of all the fingers, Movement of all tendons individually, palpate for foreign bodies, look for tracking cellulitis/ lymphangitis/ lymphadenopathy

Investigations
Bloods including inflammatory markers, group and save, glucose, urea and electrolytes, blood culture, wound culture and hand X-ray 

Initial management 
Urgent admission, intravenous antibiotics, stick hand elevation in Bradford sling,  marking area of redness with date and time recording, Consent and mark the patient, prepare for surgical washout in theatres.

Definitive management
Using arm tourniquet without exsanguination, minimal access incisions over A1 and A5 pulley. If frank pus is present, open as Brunner incision or midlateral approach. Excision of the puncture wound, taking microbiology swabs, washing by saline using a venflon cannula from proximal to distal. Ensure the potential spaces are explored and opened including the thenar, hypothenar, and mid-palmar spaces, as well as the space of Parona in the distal forearm and the web spaces if involved. In a horseshoe abscess, both little finger and thumb flexor sheaths communicate through the ulnar and radial bursae and require thorough washout of all spaces.

Post-operative plan
Splint the hand in position of safe immobilisation using a P O S I cast, hand elevation, continue antibiotics and arrange for second look in 48 hours and early hand therapy.

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
