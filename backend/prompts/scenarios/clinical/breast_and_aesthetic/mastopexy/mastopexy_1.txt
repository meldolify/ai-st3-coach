SCENARIO TITLE

Mastopexy for Post-Breastfeeding Ptosis

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A thirty-eight year old woman has been referred to your clinic with breast ptosis following breastfeeding three children. She is happy with her breast size but unhappy with the shape and position of her nipples which she feels have dropped significantly. On examination she has Regnault grade two ptosis bilaterally with the nipple sitting approximately two centimetres below the inframammary fold. Her B M I is twenty-three and she has completed her family.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Classification
Regnault grade one nipple at the level of the inframammary fold
Regnault grade two nipple below the fold but above the lowest breast point
Regnault grade three nipple at the lowest point of the breast
Pseudoptosis where nipple is above the fold but the lower pole is ptotic
This patient has grade two ptosis

Assessment
Sternal notch to nipple distance
Nipple to inframammary fold distance
Breast base width
Assessment of breast volume and whether the patient is happy with size
Skin quality and elasticity
Confirmation that the patient has completed her family
Stable weight

Technique Selection
Periareolar or circumareolar mastopexy for mild ptosis grade one with limited lift and risk of areolar widening
Vertical scar mastopexy for moderate ptosis grade one to two providing good lift with a lollipop scar
Wise pattern mastopexy for severe ptosis grade two to three providing maximum lift with an inverted T scar
For this patient with grade two ptosis a vertical scar or Wise pattern mastopexy would be appropriate

Operative Planning
New nipple position determined by the inframammary fold transposition point
Pedicle choice to maintain nipple blood supply, commonly supermedial or inferior
Mastopexy does not significantly reduce breast volume unlike a breast reduction

Consent
Scarring and scar position depending on technique
Altered nipple sensation
Recurrence of ptosis over time
Asymmetry
Wound healing complications
Potential impact on breastfeeding although she has completed her family
Need for revision

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Discussion of augmentation mastopexy as a combined procedure and the increased complexity and complication rate
Knowledge of the auto-augmentation technique using inferior pedicle deepithelialised flap to add upper pole fullness
Understanding that mastopexy results are not permanent and ptosis may recur with time, weight changes, or gravity
Discussion of internal mastopexy or mesh-supported mastopexy techniques
Knowledge of the trade-off between degree of lift achieved and length of scar
Understanding that mastopexy is primarily a cosmetic procedure and N H S funding is rarely available
Discussion of timing relative to future pregnancies and the recommendation to wait until family is complete

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
