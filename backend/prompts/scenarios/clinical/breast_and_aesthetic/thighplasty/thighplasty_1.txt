SCENARIO TITLE

Thighplasty Following Massive Weight Loss

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A forty year old woman has been referred to your clinic with excess skin on both inner thighs following massive weight loss after bariatric surgery. She lost fifty kilograms over two years and her weight has been stable for fourteen months. The excess skin causes chafing and recurrent fungal infections in the thigh creases, and she is unable to wear trousers comfortably. Her current B M I is twenty-seven.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Assessment
Weight stability for at least twelve months
Functional symptoms including chafing, intertrigo, skin infections, and difficulty with clothing
Distribution of skin excess in the medial thigh
Whether excess extends to the lateral thigh or around the full circumference
Skin quality and degree of laxity
Previous bariatric procedure and nutritional status

Surgical Options
Medial thigh lift with an incision in the groin crease for mild to moderate excess limited to the proximal inner thigh
Vertical medial thigh lift with a scar extending from the groin to the knee for extensive excess along the full length of the thigh
Lateral thigh lift or combined circumferential lower body lift for lateral and posterior thigh excess
Liposuction as an adjunct for fatty excess

Incision Planning
Groin crease incision anchored to Colles fascia to prevent scar migration
For vertical thigh lift the scar runs along the inner thigh which is the main aesthetic trade-off
Importance of anchoring the lift to a deep fascial structure to prevent descent of the scar

Important Anatomy
Lymphatic vessels of the medial thigh at risk of injury causing lymphoedema or lymphocele
Great saphenous vein and its tributaries
Femoral triangle structures
Sensory nerves to the medial thigh

Consent
Wound dehiscence which is common in this area due to tension and moisture
Scar migration inferiorly if not adequately anchored
Lymphoedema or lymphocele
Asymmetry
Seroma
Altered sensation in the inner thigh
Visible scarring particularly with the vertical approach
Recurrence of laxity
Labial distortion from excessive traction

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Discussion of the Pittsburgh classification of thigh deformities after weight loss
Knowledge of the fascial anchoring technique to Colles fascia or the deep fascia of the thigh
Understanding of the spiral thigh lift as described by Le Louarn
Discussion of staging thighplasty with other body contouring procedures
Knowledge of quilting sutures to reduce dead space and seroma risk
Understanding that thighplasty has one of the highest complication rates of all body contouring procedures
Discussion of compression garments and activity restrictions postoperatively

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
