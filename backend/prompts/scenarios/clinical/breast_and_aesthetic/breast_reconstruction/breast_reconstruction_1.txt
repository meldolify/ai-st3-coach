SCENARIO TITLE

Breast Reconstruction Following Mastectomy

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A fifty-two year old woman has been diagnosed with invasive ductal carcinoma of the left breast and is planned for mastectomy. She has been referred to you by the breast surgeon to discuss her options for breast reconstruction. She is a non-smoker with a B M I of twenty-seven and has no significant comorbidities. She would like to understand the different approaches available to her.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Patient Assessment
Body habitus and available donor tissue
Smoking status and B M I
Comorbidities affecting fitness for prolonged surgery
Previous abdominal surgery which may preclude abdominal flaps
Patient wishes and expectations
Oncological treatment plan including need for radiotherapy

Timing
Immediate reconstruction at the time of mastectomy
Delayed reconstruction after completion of adjuvant treatment
Delayed-immediate reconstruction with tissue expander placed at mastectomy then definitive reconstruction later
Impact of radiotherapy on reconstruction outcomes particularly with implants

Implant-Based Reconstruction
Subpectoral or prepectoral placement
Two-stage with tissue expander followed by definitive implant
One-stage direct to implant with or without acellular dermal matrix
Advantages include shorter operation and no donor site morbidity
Disadvantages include higher rates of capsular contracture especially after radiotherapy and the need for implant replacement

Autologous Reconstruction
D I E P flap as the gold standard for autologous reconstruction using deep inferior epigastric perforator vessels
Latissimus dorsi flap with or without an implant
T R A M flap and the difference between free and pedicled
Other options including S G A P, T M G, and profunda artery perforator flaps
Advantages of autologous tissue including a more natural result that ages with the patient

Symmetrisation
Contralateral breast may need reduction, augmentation, or mastopexy to achieve symmetry
This is usually performed as a secondary procedure

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Knowledge of C T angiography for preoperative perforator mapping before D I E P flap
Discussion of the role of lipofilling for contour refinement
Understanding of oncoplastic breast surgery as an alternative to mastectomy plus reconstruction
Knowledge of prepectoral implant reconstruction and the role of acellular dermal matrix wraps
Discussion of the psychological benefits of immediate reconstruction
Understanding of the N I C E guidelines on immediate reconstruction
Knowledge of nipple reconstruction techniques including local flaps and tattooing
Discussion of autologous fat transfer for volume supplementation
Understanding of the impact of B R C A gene mutations on reconstructive planning including risk-reducing contralateral mastectomy

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
