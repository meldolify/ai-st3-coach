SCENARIO TITLE

Brachioplasty Following Massive Weight Loss

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A forty-five year old woman has been referred to your clinic with excess skin on both upper arms following significant weight loss. She lost forty kilograms after a gastric bypass two years ago and her weight has been stable for over twelve months. The excess skin hangs down when she extends her arms and she finds it very difficult to wear short-sleeved clothing. Her B M I is twenty-nine.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Assessment
Weight stability for at least twelve months
Current B M I assessment
Distribution of skin excess along the length of the arm
Whether excess extends into the axilla or lateral chest wall
Skin quality and elasticity
Previous weight loss method and nutritional status
Assessment of other body areas with excess skin for staged procedures

Surgical Options
Liposuction alone for patients with good skin elasticity and predominantly fatty excess rather than skin excess
Mini brachioplasty or short scar brachioplasty for excess limited to the proximal arm near the axilla
Full brachioplasty with an incision from the axilla to the medial epicondyle for extensive skin excess
Incision placed along the medial bicipital groove to hide the scar on the inner arm

Important Anatomy
Medial cutaneous nerve of the forearm which runs in the subcutaneous tissue and is at risk during dissection
Basilic vein which lies in the medial arm
Medial cutaneous nerve of the arm
Lymphatics of the medial arm
Ulnar nerve at the medial epicondyle

Consent
Visible scarring which is the most significant concern as the scar is long and often visible
Wound dehiscence
Seroma
Haematoma
Altered sensation in the forearm from medial cutaneous nerve injury
Asymmetry
Scar widening
Lymphoedema of the hand or forearm
Need for revision

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Knowledge of the L or Z plasty extension into the axilla for patients with axillary skin excess
Discussion of combining brachioplasty with lateral chest wall skin excision
Understanding of fascial suspension techniques to reduce scar widening
Knowledge of liposuction as an adjunct to skin excision
Discussion of the fish-shaped or elliptical excision pattern
Understanding of the staging of body contouring procedures following massive weight loss
Knowledge of compression garment use postoperatively
Discussion of the trade-off between scar length and degree of correction that patients must understand

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
