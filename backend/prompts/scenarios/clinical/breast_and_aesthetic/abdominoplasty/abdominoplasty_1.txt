SCENARIO TITLE

Abdominoplasty Following Massive Weight Loss

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A forty-two year old woman has been referred to your clinic following massive weight loss after bariatric surgery. She has lost sixty kilograms over the past two years and now has a large overhanging pannus causing recurrent intertrigo and skin infections. Her weight has been stable for the last twelve months and her current B M I is twenty-eight. She is keen to discuss surgical options for removing the excess skin.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

History and Patient Selection
Weight loss history and method of weight loss
Weight stability for at least twelve months before surgery
Current B M I ideally below thirty-five
Nutritional status following bariatric surgery
Functional symptoms from pannus including intertrigo, skin infections, difficulty with hygiene and mobility
Smoking status and impact on wound healing
Previous abdominal surgery and scars

Examination
Degree and distribution of skin excess
Assessment for diastasis recti
Assessment for abdominal wall hernias including incisional and umbilical
Skin quality and degree of striae
Distribution of excess in vertical and horizontal planes

Surgical Technique
Standard abdominoplasty with low transverse incision for predominantly horizontal skin excess
Fleur de Lys abdominoplasty adding a vertical midline excision for patients with significant vertical and horizontal skin excess as commonly seen after massive weight loss
Umbilical transposition through the abdominal flap
Plication of the rectus diastasis if present
Hernia repair can be performed simultaneously

Consent
Wound infection and dehiscence
Seroma which is the most common complication
Haematoma
Skin flap necrosis particularly in smokers or patients with high B M I
Altered sensation of the abdominal wall
Umbilical necrosis or malposition
V T E risk and need for thromboprophylaxis
Revision surgery
Dog ears at the lateral extent of the scar

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Understanding of the post-bariatric body contouring sequence and staging of procedures
Knowledge of circumferential body lift or belt lipectomy as an alternative for circumferential excess
Discussion of progressive tension sutures or quilting sutures to reduce seroma
Knowledge of Huger zones of abdominal wall blood supply and implications for flap viability
Discussion of combined procedures and increased complication rates
Understanding of the difference between functional abdominoplasty for pannus and cosmetic abdominoplasty
Knowledge of the role of liposuction as an adjunct and the caution with undermined flaps
Discussion of enhanced recovery protocols for body contouring surgery

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
