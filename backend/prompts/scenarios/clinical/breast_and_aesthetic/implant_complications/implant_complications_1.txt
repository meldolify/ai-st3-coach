SCENARIO TITLE

Breast Implant Rupture and B I A dash A L C L

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A fifty year old woman presents to your clinic concerned about a change in the shape of her right breast. She had bilateral silicone breast augmentation with textured implants fifteen years ago. Over the last three months the right breast has become swollen and she has noticed a new fluid collection around it. The left breast is unchanged. She is otherwise well with no constitutional symptoms.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Differential Diagnosis
Implant rupture either intracapsular or extracapsular
Late-onset seroma which should raise suspicion of B I A dash A L C L
Capsular contracture
Infection
Breast cancer unrelated to the implant

Implant Rupture
Intracapsular rupture where the implant shell fails but silicone is contained within the capsule
Extracapsular rupture where silicone has leaked beyond the capsule into surrounding tissues
M R I is the gold standard for diagnosing silicone implant rupture
The linguine sign on M R I is characteristic of intracapsular rupture
Management of rupture involves surgical removal of the implant and capsulectomy

B I A dash A L C L
Breast implant associated anaplastic large cell lymphoma
Associated with textured surface implants
Typically presents as a late-onset periprosthetic seroma more than one year after implantation
Much less commonly presents as a mass or capsular contracture
Incidence is low but clinicians must have a high index of suspicion

B I A dash A L C L Investigations
Ultrasound-guided aspiration of the periprosthetic fluid
Cytology of the aspirated fluid for atypical cells
C D 30 immunohistochemistry which is positive in B I A dash A L C L
If confirmed then staging with P E T dash C T
Referral to a lymphoma M D T

B I A dash A L C L Management
Total capsulectomy with implant removal as the primary treatment
En bloc resection if possible
Most cases are confined to the capsule and have an excellent prognosis with surgery alone
If there is a mass or lymph node involvement then oncological input for chemotherapy is required
N I C E and B A P R A S guidance should be followed

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Knowledge of the M H R A yellow card reporting for breast implant complications
Discussion of the different implant surfaces including smooth, microtextured, macrotextured, and polyurethane and their relative risk
Understanding of the breast implant registry and its role in surveillance
Knowledge of the P I P implant scandal and its implications for implant regulation
Discussion of silicone granulomas in extracapsular rupture
Understanding of the Ann Arbor staging system adapted for B I A dash A L C L
Knowledge that the vast majority of B I A dash A L C L cases are stage one E confined to the capsule with greater than ninety percent five-year survival
Discussion of patient counselling regarding implant removal with or without replacement and the option of autologous reconstruction
Understanding of the role of the breast implant check service
