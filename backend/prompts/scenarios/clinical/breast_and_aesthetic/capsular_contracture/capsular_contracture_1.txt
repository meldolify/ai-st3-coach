SCENARIO TITLE

Capsular Contracture Following Breast Augmentation

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A forty-five year old woman presents to your clinic with a painful and firm left breast two years after bilateral breast augmentation with silicone implants. The left breast has become progressively harder and has changed shape over the last six months. On examination the left breast is firm, tender, and appears higher and rounder than the right. The right breast is soft and has a normal appearance.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Diagnosis and Classification
Baker classification: grade one the breast is normally soft, grade two the breast is slightly firm but looks normal, grade three the breast is firm and looks abnormal, grade four the breast is hard, painful, and looks abnormal
This patient has Baker grade three or four capsular contracture

Risk Factors
Subclinical infection and biofilm formation
Haematoma
Subglandular implant placement has higher rates than submuscular
Smooth implants compared to textured although textured carry B I A dash A L C L risk
Radiotherapy
Silicone gel bleed

Investigations
Ultrasound to assess implant integrity and rule out rupture
M R I which is the gold standard for detecting silicone implant rupture with the linguine sign for intracapsular rupture
Consideration of seroma or late-onset periprosthetic fluid collection which should raise concern for B I A dash A L C L

Surgical Management
Capsulotomy which is release of the capsule by scoring or dividing it, used for milder contracture
Open capsulectomy which is partial or total removal of the capsule
Total capsulectomy or en bloc capsulectomy removing the entire capsule with the implant
Implant exchange with change of pocket plane for example subglandular to submuscular
Implant removal without replacement if the patient wishes

Prevention of Recurrence
Change of pocket plane
Use of textured implants though this must be balanced against B I A dash A L C L risk
Meticulous haemostasis
Antibiotic irrigation of the pocket
Minimal implant handling and the fourteen-point plan
Consideration of acellular dermal matrix as a pocket lining

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Knowledge of the fourteen-point plan for reducing capsular contracture
Discussion of the role of leukotriene receptor antagonists such as montelukast in early capsular contracture
Understanding of the biofilm theory of capsular contracture and the role of the Keller funnel in no-touch technique
Discussion of B I A dash A L C L including its association with textured implants, presentation as late seroma, and the need for C D 30 immunohistochemistry and cytology of periprosthetic fluid
Knowledge of the difference between intracapsular and extracapsular rupture and their management
Discussion of fat transfer as an alternative to reimplantation
Understanding of the M H R A recommendations regarding breast implant surveillance

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
