STAGE PROMPT QUESTIONS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud verbatim.
Use these to guide prompting. Delivery specificity is controlled by the personality layer.)

[AUTHOR NOTE — DELETE THIS ENTIRE SECTION WHEN FINISHED

These are the questions the examiner can ask the candidate during each stage. They are used to guide the candidate if they stall or give incomplete answers.

Write two to four questions per stage. Each question should target a specific essential or bonus marking point without naming it directly.

Format them under stage headings exactly like this:

Assessment
"What would you expect to find on examination of this patient?"
"Can you describe the A B C D E approach for this case?"

Investigations
"What blood tests would you want to send?"
"Are there any scoring systems that might be relevant here?"

Initial Management
"How would you stabilise this patient initially?"
"What antibiotics would you consider and why?"

Definitive Management
"What surgical intervention does this patient need?"
"Can you walk me through the key steps of the operation?"

Not every stage needs to be included. If a stage is not relevant to this case, leave it out. For example, some cases may not need an Investigations stage.

END OF AUTHOR NOTE]

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

[AUTHOR NOTE — DELETE THIS ENTIRE SECTION WHEN FINISHED

List the critical points the candidate MUST cover to pass. Group them under the same domain headings used above.

Each point should be a short statement of the expected answer.

Example from Necrotising Fasciitis:

Diagnosis and urgency
Necrotising fasciitis which is a clinical emergency.

Assessment
Must include vital status, rapid progression or spread, and poorly controlled diabetes.

Investigations
Blood tests including lactate level.

Initial management
Stabilisation and resuscitation with I V fluids, and antibiotics as per the guidelines.

Definitive management
Urgent surgery for debridement.

Surgical steps
Surgical debridement of all unhealthy tissue.

Post-operative care
I T U post-operatively, and arranging for a potential second look within forty eight hours.

END OF AUTHOR NOTE]

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

[AUTHOR NOTE — DELETE THIS ENTIRE SECTION WHEN FINISHED

List additional points that earn extra credit. These demonstrate deeper knowledge beyond the essentials.

Group them under the same domain headings. Each point should be a short statement.

Example from Necrotising Fasciitis:

Diagnosis and urgency
Differential diagnosis including cellulitis, but treating this as necrotising fasciitis until proven otherwise.

Assessment
Mentioning Sepsis six.
Examination of spread of erythema beyond previous markings.

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
