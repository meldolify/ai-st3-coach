SCENARIO TITLE

Rhinoplasty Assessment

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A twenty-eight year old woman has been referred to your clinic because she is unhappy with the appearance of her nose. She describes a dorsal hump and feels her nose is too wide. She also reports some difficulty breathing through the left side of her nose. She has no history of nasal trauma and has not had any previous nasal surgery.

STAGE PROMPT QUESTIONS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud verbatim.
Use these to guide prompting. Delivery specificity is controlled by the personality layer.)

Assessment
"How would you take a history from this patient?"
"What specific questions would you ask about the functional component?"
"How would you examine this patient's nose?"
"What are the key views you would assess and what are you looking for in each?"
"How would you assess nasal airway obstruction?"

Investigations
"Would you arrange any investigations for this patient?"
"Is there a role for photography and if so what views would you take?"

Initial Management
"How would you manage the functional component of her complaint?"
"Would you refer this patient to anyone else?"
"What is the role of a cosmetic tribunal or panel in this case?"

Definitive Management
"What are the differences between open and closed rhinoplasty?"
"How would you address the dorsal hump?"
"How would you narrow a wide nose?"
"What are the key risks you would discuss with this patient?"

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

History
Specific cosmetic concerns and patient expectations
Functional symptoms including nasal obstruction, which side, and duration
History of cocaine or nasal decongestant use
Previous nasal trauma or surgery
Allergies and rhinitis symptoms
Psychological assessment and body dysmorphic disorder screening
Motivation for surgery

Examination
Frontal view assessing symmetry, width, and deviation
Lateral view assessing dorsal profile, nasal length, tip projection, and the nasofrontal angle
Basal view assessing nostril symmetry, columellar show, and tip shape
Internal examination for septal deviation, turbinate hypertrophy, and mucosal condition
Cottle test for internal nasal valve collapse
Skin thickness and quality assessment

Functional Assessment
Cottle test where lateral traction on the cheek improves airflow suggesting internal valve collapse
Assessment of the nasal septum for deviation
Referral to E N T if significant functional obstruction for septoplasty consideration

Surgical Approach
Open rhinoplasty via a transcolumellar incision gives better exposure for complex work
Closed rhinoplasty via endonasal incisions has no external scar and less tip oedema
Dorsal hump reduction by rasping or component reduction of bone and cartilage
Lateral osteotomies to narrow a wide bony vault
Tip refinement with suture techniques or cartilage grafts

Consent
Bleeding
Infection
Asymmetry
Need for revision surgery which occurs in five to fifteen percent
Altered sensation
Nasal obstruction
Failure to meet expectations
Scarring with open approach

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Knowledge of the nasal aesthetic subunits and their relevance to surgical planning
Understanding of the tripod concept of nasal tip support
Discussion of spreader grafts for internal nasal valve reconstruction
Knowledge of alar base reduction techniques
Discussion of the role of computer imaging in managing patient expectations
Understanding of the cosmetic surgery governance process including cooling off period
Discussion of secondary rhinoplasty and the increased complexity of revision surgery
Knowledge of the difference between a cosmetic tribunal and a standard M D T
Understanding of autologous cartilage graft sources including septum, ear, and rib

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
