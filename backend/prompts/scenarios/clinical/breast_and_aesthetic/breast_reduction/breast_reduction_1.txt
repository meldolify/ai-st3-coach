SCENARIO TITLE

Bilateral Breast Reduction for Symptomatic Macromastia

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

A forty-eight year old woman has been referred to your clinic with symptoms of back pain, neck pain and shoulder grooving from her bra straps. She describes difficulty exercising and finding clothes that fit. On examination she has Regnault grade three ptosis bilaterally with a sternal notch to nipple distance of thirty-six centimetres. Her B M I is thirty-one.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

History and Symptoms
Functional symptoms including back pain, neck pain, shoulder grooving, intertrigo, difficulty exercising
Psychological impact and effect on quality of life
B M I assessment and whether weight optimisation should be attempted first

Classification of Ptosis
Regnault classification: grade one nipple at the level of the inframammary fold, grade two nipple below the fold but above the lowest point of the breast, grade three nipple at the lowest point of the breast
Pseudoptosis where nipple remains above the fold but breast tissue hangs below

Clinical Measurements
Sternal notch to nipple distance
Nipple to inframammary fold distance
Breast base width
Understanding that sternal notch to nipple distance guides technique selection

Surgical Technique
Wise pattern with inverted T scar for this degree of ptosis and distance
Sternal notch to nipple less than thirty centimetres in a young patient with good skin may allow vertical scar technique
Thirty to forty centimetres generally suitable for Wise pattern with pedicled nipple
Greater than forty centimetres raises concern about nipple viability on a pedicle and may require free nipple graft

Pedicle Choice
Supermedial pedicle or inferior pedicle for Wise pattern reduction
Understanding the blood supply to the nipple areola complex on the chosen pedicle

Consent
Scarring in an inverted T pattern
Altered nipple sensation
Reduced or absent ability to breastfeed
Asymmetry
Wound dehiscence particularly at the T junction
Fat necrosis
Nipple necrosis risk especially with longer pedicles
Need for revision surgery

BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Discussion of supermedial pedicle versus inferior pedicle advantages including better projection with supermedial
Penn formula for nipple position using breast meridian and inframammary fold measurements
Knowledge of vertical scar techniques such as Lassus or Lejour and appropriate patient selection
Understanding of N H S commissioning criteria and typical B M I thresholds
Discussion of specimen histology and the incidence of incidental malignancy findings
Mention of liposuction as an adjunct to the lateral breast tissue
Knowledge of minimum tissue weight removed for funding approval typically three hundred grams per side
Discussion of postoperative bra use and activity restrictions

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.
