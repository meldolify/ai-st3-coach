SCENARIO TITLE

Replantation

CASE INTRODUCTION

(Read verbatim in the Case Introduction state.)

This is a 24 year old heavy smoker who is currently in ED department after sustaining a circular saw injury to his right thumb. You are on call and have been asked to review this patient.

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS
(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Diagnosis and urgency
Thumb amputation which is a clinical emergency.

Assessment, either through A B C D E approach or history and examination
The primary survey includes making sure that the patient is hemodynamically stable and there is no associated life threatening injuries. Then taking a focused history from the patient including The mechanism of injury and the timing since the injury. asking how the amputated part was preserved before arrival to the hospital.
Examination is divided into examining the hand as a whole, as well as examining the amputated part to confirm the level of amputation.

Investigations
X-ray of the hand and the amputated part.

Initial management
Analgesia, Antibiotics, preservation of the amputated part.
Consenting the patient for a trial at replantation in addition to shortening and direct closure. Discussion with the on call consultant and the consultant anaesthetist to arrange urgent take to theatre.

Definitive management

Surgical steps
Under general anesthesia with an arm tourniquet. Exploration of the amputated part and tagging the structures, and then exploration of the hand in the same fashion.
The order of repair begins with Skeletal stabilisation, Repair of the deeper tendon structures then vessel repair and lastly nerve repair.

Post-operative care
Hand elevation, Keeping the hand warm, hourly obs on the ward, hand therapy in 3-5 days.


BONUS MARKING POINTS

(Internal reference only. Do not read aloud.)

Diagnosis 
Stating that complete finger amputation is a plastic surgery emergency.

Assessment, either through A B C D E approach or history and examination, and mentioning A T L S principles. Confirming that there are no other associated life-threatening injuries.
Taking a focused history including age, hand, dominance, occupation, hobbies. The mechanism of injury and the timing since the injury in addition to the degree of contamination. Asking about the past medical history, drug history, and commenting on the fact that the patient is a heavy smoker which can affect the outcome. last meal. Asking how the part was preserved prior to arrival at the hospital (Ideally it should be wrapped in gauze, then put in a plastic bag, and then put in another bag with ice. Acknowledging ischaemia time and knowing the difference between warm ischaemia time (12 hours) and cold ischaemia time (up to 24 hours).
Examination is divided into examining the hand and the amputated part to confirm the level of amputation and the degree of contamination. Look for tendon or vessel avulsion like the ribbon sign. Examination of the rest of the hand to identify any associated injuries of the hand.

Investigations
Blood tests including group and save and cross-matched blood to prepare for surgery. X-ray of the hand and the amputated part.

Initial management
Stopping any bleeding, Analgesia, Tetanus, Antibiotics, preservation of the amputated part, irrigation and dressing, hand elevation.


Definitive management
Consenting the patient for a trial at replantation, tendon grafts, nerve grafts, vein grafts in addition to shortening and direct closure. Discussion with the on call consultant, the consultant anaesthetist, the theatre coordinator and the bed manager for the ward.

Surgical steps
General anaesthetic.
Patient positioning, supine in this case with arm table and arm tourniquet
The needed instruments and sets are: microscope, c-arm machine, hand set, K-wire set and micro set
While the patient is in the anaesthetic room, starty by exploration of the amputated part, looking for a suitable artery and vein and tagging of the identified structures.
Once the patient is anaesthetised exploration of the hand in the same fashion. 
Osteosynthesis or shortening of the bone may sometimes be required
Skeletal fixation using K-wires
Repair of the tendons first using 4 strand technique then either vessels then nerves or vice versa.

Post-operative care
Hand elevation
Keeping the hand warm
Monitor the urine output with the aim of at least 0.5 ml/Kg/hour
hourly finger obs
hand therapy to begin within 3-5 days
Monitoring for signs of arterial ischaemia or venous congestion
