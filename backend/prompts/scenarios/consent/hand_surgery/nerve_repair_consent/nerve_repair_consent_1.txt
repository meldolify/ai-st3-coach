SCENARIO TITLE

Nerve Repair Consent

PATIENT QUESTIONS

(Internal reference only. Questions the patient or patient's relative may ask during the consent discussion.
Use these after the candidate finishes their explanation.
How anxious or questioning you are is controlled by the personality layer.)

[AUTHOR NOTE — DELETE THIS ENTIRE SECTION WHEN FINISHED

Write 6-10 patient questions that would naturally arise during this consent discussion.
These should be written in everyday language as a real patient would ask.

Examples for carpal tunnel release:
"Will I be awake during it?"
"How long does the operation take?"
"When can I go back to work?"
"You said there's a risk of infection — how common is that?"
"Will the numbness definitely go away?"
"What about the scar — will it be very noticeable?"
"When do I need to come back to get checked?"

For paediatric scenarios, write questions as a parent:
"Will my child be in pain afterwards?"
"How long will the anaesthetic take to wear off?"
"When can they go back to school?"

END OF AUTHOR NOTE]

MAIN CONSENT DOMAINS

[AUTHOR NOTE — DELETE THIS ENTIRE SECTION WHEN FINISHED

List the 4-6 main consent domains being assessed in this scenario.

Example for carpal tunnel release:
Introduction and patient identification
Procedure explanation (what will be done, in layman terms)
Anaesthesia (local vs general, what to expect)
Post-operative care (dressings, restrictions, follow-up)
Risks and complications (infection, bleeding, nerve damage, recurrence, scar)
Recovery and return to activities

END OF AUTHOR NOTE]
