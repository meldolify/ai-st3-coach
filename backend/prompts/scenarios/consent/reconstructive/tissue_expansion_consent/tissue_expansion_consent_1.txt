SCENARIO TITLE

Tissue Expander Insertion for Scalp Reconstruction Consent

(Internal reference only. Questions the patient or patient's relative may ask during the consent discussion.

"Will I be asleep for the operation?"
"What does the expander look like — is it like a balloon?"
"How long does the whole process take from start to finish?"
"Will I look strange during the expansion — will people notice?"
"Does the expansion hurt?"

MAIN CONSENT DOMAINS

Introduction and patient identification
Procedure explanation including expander insertion, expansion process, and second stage reconstruction
Anaesthesia and operative environment
Post-operative care and expansion schedule
Risks and complications including expander-specific risks
Recovery timeline and two-stage process

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

Introduction and patient identification
Confirming the patient's identity and the planned procedure.
Establishing rapport and checking the patient's current understanding of the two-stage process.
Acknowledging the psychological impact of the alopecia scar and the patient's goals for reconstruction.

Procedure explanation
Explaining in lay terms that the operation has two stages: today a silicone balloon called a tissue expander is placed under the normal hair-bearing skin next to the bald scar through a small cut.
Explaining that over the following weeks the expander is gradually filled with salt water through a small valve called a port, accessed by a needle through the skin, which stretches the overlying skin.
Explaining that the expansion process creates extra skin that will eventually be used to cover the area of scarring once the scar tissue is cut out in the second operation.
Explaining that the second operation involves removing the expander and the scar tissue, then advancing the expanded skin to cover the defect and closing it with stitches.
Mentioning the approximate duration of today's operation, typically one to one and a half hours.

Anaesthesia and operative environment
Explaining that today's procedure is performed under general anaesthetic.
Mentioning that the patient will meet the anaesthetist beforehand.
Explaining the sterile theatre environment.

Post-operative care and expansion schedule
Explaining that a drain may be left in place and removed before discharge.
Advising that the patient should expect to stay in hospital for one night.
Explaining that expansion typically begins two to three weeks after the first operation and involves clinic visits every one to two weeks.
Explaining that each expansion visit involves injecting salt water through the skin into the expander using a needle, which may cause a feeling of tightness or mild discomfort that settles within a day.
Explaining that the expansion process typically takes two to three months depending on the amount of skin needed.

Risks and complications
Mentioning bleeding and haematoma.
Mentioning infection, noting that infection around a foreign body like an expander may be difficult to treat with antibiotics alone and may require removal of the device.
Mentioning expander exposure or extrusion where the device comes through the skin, requiring removal.
Mentioning expander failure or deflation.
Mentioning pain or discomfort during the expansion process.
Mentioning wound breakdown at the incision site.
Mentioning scarring.
Mentioning altered sensation or numbness in the expanded skin.
Mentioning asymmetry or an unsatisfactory cosmetic result.
Mentioning the possibility that the expanded skin may not be sufficient to cover the entire defect, requiring further procedures.

Recovery timeline
Advising avoidance of contact sports and vigorous activity during the expansion period to protect the expander.
Advising return to sedentary work at approximately one to two weeks after the first operation.
Explaining the altered appearance during expansion and that this is temporary.
Explaining that the final cosmetic result will not be apparent until several months after the second operation.

BONUS MARKING POINTS

Procedure explanation
Explaining the biological principle of tissue expansion: the body responds to sustained mechanical stretch by generating new skin cells, a process called biological creep, producing genuinely new tissue rather than just stretching existing skin.
Mentioning that expanded skin in the scalp will grow hair normally because the hair follicles are preserved and duplicated during expansion.
Discussing the shape and size options for expanders, such as rectangular, crescent, or custom shapes, chosen to match the defect.

Risks and complications
Mentioning bone remodelling or thinning of the underlying calvarium with prolonged expansion, which is usually reversible.
Mentioning the psychological impact of the altered appearance during expansion and the importance of psychological preparation.
Discussing the risk of widened scars if there is excessive tension on the final closure.
Mentioning the possibility of serial tissue expansion if the defect is too large for a single expansion cycle.

Post-operative care
Explaining that analgesics may be needed after expansion sessions and that mild discomfort is expected.
Mentioning the importance of protecting the expander from trauma.

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.

END OF SCENARIO CRITERIA
