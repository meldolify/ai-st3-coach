SCENARIO TITLE

Scar Revision Consent

PATIENT QUESTIONS

(Internal reference only. Questions the patient or patient's relative may ask during the consent discussion.
Use these after the candidate finishes their explanation.
How anxious or questioning you are is controlled by the personality layer.)

"Will the new scar be completely invisible?"
"Will I be awake during it?"
"Why can't you just cut it out and make it disappear?"
"How long does the operation take?"
"When will I see the final result?"
"Is there a chance it could end up looking worse than it does now?"
"Will I need to use any creams or treatments on the scar afterwards?"
"You mentioned Z-plasty — what does that mean exactly?"
"When can I go back to the gym?"

MAIN CONSENT DOMAINS

Introduction and patient identification
Procedure explanation including excision technique and closure
Anaesthesia and pain management
Post-operative care and scar management
Risks and complications
Recovery timeline and realistic expectations

ESSENTIAL MARKING POINTS

Introduction and patient identification
Confirming the patient's identity and the planned procedure.
Establishing rapport and exploring the patient's concerns about the current scar and what she hopes to achieve.
Setting realistic expectations from the outset: explaining that scar revision improves a scar but cannot make it disappear entirely.

Procedure explanation
Explaining in lay terms that the operation involves cutting out the old scar and closing the wound with careful stitching techniques designed to produce a finer, flatter scar.
Explaining that the new scar will be at least as long as the old one and may be slightly longer depending on the technique used.
Mentioning that techniques such as Z-plasty may be used to reorient the scar along natural skin creases, explaining this in lay terms as a zigzag cut that helps the scar blend in better.
Mentioning that any tethering of the scar to deeper tissues will be released.
Explaining the approximate duration of surgery, typically 30 minutes to one hour.

Anaesthesia and pain management
Explaining that the procedure is performed under local anaesthetic with the patient awake.
Explaining the injection process and reassuring the patient that the initial sting settles quickly and there should be no sharp pain during the procedure.

Post-operative care and scar management
Explaining that the wound will be closed with fine stitches and possibly also deeper stitches beneath the skin.
Explaining that stitches will be removed at approximately ten to fourteen days.
Advising the patient to keep the wound clean and dry until stitches are removed.
Explaining that scar management measures will be important: silicone gel or sheeting, scar massage, and sun protection.
Explaining that the new scar will go through a maturation process over twelve to eighteen months during which it may initially look red and raised before fading.

Risks and complications
Mentioning bleeding and bruising.
Mentioning infection.
Mentioning wound breakdown.
Mentioning hypertrophic or keloid scarring, explaining that the new scar could potentially become raised or thickened again, similar to the original.
Mentioning that the scar could look the same as or worse than the original scar.
Mentioning altered sensation or numbness around the scar.
Mentioning asymmetry or contour irregularity.
Mentioning the possibility of needing further revision procedures.

Recovery timeline
Advising avoidance of strenuous exercise and heavy lifting for approximately four weeks to reduce tension on the wound.
Advising return to normal activities at approximately two weeks.
Emphasising that the final result takes twelve to eighteen months to mature and patience is needed.
Explaining that compliance with scar management is important for the best result.

BONUS MARKING POINTS

Procedure explanation
Explaining the principles of scar revision: excising the old scar, undermining the surrounding tissue, meticulous layered closure, and reorienting the scar to lie within relaxed skin tension lines.
Mentioning alternative or adjunctive treatments such as steroid injection, fat grafting, laser therapy, or dermal substitutes, and when these might be considered.
Explaining why the original scar formed poorly: wound tension, infection, or the scar crossing skin tension lines.

Risks and complications
Discussing patient-specific factors that affect scarring: skin type, ethnicity, age, location on the body, and genetic predisposition to keloid formation.
Mentioning that darker skin types have a higher tendency to form keloid or hypertrophic scars.
Mentioning the psychological impact if the result does not meet expectations and the importance of counselling.

Post-operative care
Explaining the evidence for silicone-based products in scar management.
Mentioning the role of pressure garments for hypertrophic scars.
Advising avoidance of sun exposure to the scar for at least twelve months.

These are points a strong candidate would mention but are not required to pass.

END OF SCENARIO CRITERIA

Once the main consent domains have been reasonably covered, or you judge that the scenario time has elapsed, end the scenario naturally.
